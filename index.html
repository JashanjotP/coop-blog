Introduction

I was offered a position to work at Ontario Power Generation as a Co-op Student. During my 4-month co-op at Ontario Power Generation (part of an 8-month term), I gained valuable experience working with a collaborative team on several key projects. While I can't go into specifics due to the confidential nature of the work, this experience allowed me to develop new skills and deepen my understanding of the industry.

Information about the Employer

Ontario Power Generation (OPG) is a leading energy provider in Ontario, responsible for generating nearly half of the province’s electricity. OPG uses hydro, nuclear, and renewable energy to power homes and businesses. I am working in the nuclear sector, contributing to the company’s mission of producing clean and reliable energy.

Co-op Goals

  • Goal 1: Oral Communication

    I planned to dive into conversations with stakeholders and team members, using technical terms confidently and clearly. Success was all about getting feedback from my manager and improving on how I explained things.

  • Goal 2: Time Management

    I took charge of setting up meetings and keeping tasks on track. I measured success by how well I hit deadlines and adapted my approach based on any challenges I faced.

  • Goal 3: Learning C#

    I worked through tutorials, asked for advice, and practiced by building projects. Success meant completing a C# project that showed I knew what I was doing.

  • Goal 4: Team Work

    I focused on contributing ideas, helping out teammates, and meeting deadlines. Success was about positive feedback from the team and smoother project outcomes.

My Experience

During my co-op at Ontario Power Generation (OPG), I was part of the Operation Support Department where I played a key role in developing various internal tools. My responsibilities included creating scripts to automate processes and designing, developing, and deploying web applications tailored to meet departmental needs. This hands-on experience allowed me to hone my technical skills and contribute to the efficiency of our operations. I have provided a generic description of the projects that I have worked with and the technologies used.

Projects

Project 1: Incident Reporting System

Skills Used: TypeScript, Angular, C#, Entity Framework, Azure Blob Storage, Azure App Service

Developed a full stack incident reporting system that allowed users to upload files, search through incidents using various filters, and autogenerate PDFs from form submissions. The application was containerized and deployed using Azure App Service. The frontend was built with Angular, and the backend was implemented in C# with Entity Framework as the ORM.

Project 2: Internal Reporting Dashboard

Skills Used: TypeScript, Angular, C#, Entity Framework

Developed a dashboard for internal reporting that aggregated data from various sources into a single interface. This project involved designing the UI/UX, creating RESTful APIs, and integrating with SQL databases for data retrieval and manipulation.

Project 3: Automated Email System

Skills Used: SQL, C#, SMTP, Azure VM

Developed an automated email system that performs SQL queries to process data, generates reports, and sends them via email using SMTP. This system was deployed on an Azure VM, providing a robust and scalable solution for regular reporting and notifications.

Conclusion

I have given an overview of my four months of co-ops at OPG in my blog. I can't get into details because of confidentiality, but this experience has given me important insights into the energy industry, especially the nuclear division. Working with an experienced team and making a valuable contribution to projects has been a fulfilling experience.
